## Response being evaluated

`d-dimer.md` was split into two sub-150-word responses.

**Response A** (Study population, Findings table, multivariable regression):

> Measured in a cohort where all subjects already have "pernicious placenta previa" (PPP) — placenta previa plus a prior cesarean scar — as the inclusion criterion; PAS is assessed as a complication superimposed on this previa population, comparing PAS-complicating-PPP (n=66) against PPP-without-PAS (n=34).
>
> Findings: 2nd trimester (13–28 wk) — Elevated, AUC 0.784, sens 72.73%, spec 76.47%. 3rd trimester (>28 wk) — Further elevated, AUC 0.839, sens 78.79%, spec 79.41%.
>
> In multivariable logistic regression, D-dimer was an independent predictor (OR 2.39, 95% CI 1.43–3.99, p=0.001) alongside fibrinogen, vascularization index (VI), and vascularization-flow index (VFI); flow index (FI) was not independently significant. In a 3rd-trimester severity subgroup analysis, D-dimer was highest in percreta and lowest in accreta.

**Response B** (Combined diagnostic model, Diagnosis and design):

> D-dimer, fibrinogen, and 3D Doppler indices (VI, VFI) combined reach AUC 0.952 (95% CI 0.91–0.99), sens 90.91%, spec 91.18%, at the 3rd-trimester timepoint — the best combined diagnostic model found anywhere in this wiki's corpus.
>
> Previa confirmed by transvaginal 3D ultrasound; PAS reference standard is intraoperative findings and/or postoperative histopathology based on depth of villous invasion. Prospective cohort, formal a priori sample-size calculation (target AUC 0.90, 90% power). The authors explicitly flag that "the combined diagnostic model was developed and evaluated within the same cohort" — no external validation has yet been performed.
