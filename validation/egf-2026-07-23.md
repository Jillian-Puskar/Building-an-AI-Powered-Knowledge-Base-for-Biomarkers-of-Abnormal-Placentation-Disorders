## Response being evaluated

`egf.md` was split into two sub-150-word responses (page exceeds ~150 words as a whole). This report covers **Response B** only (mechanistic validation + combined model).

> Tissue-level validation (IHC/RT-qPCR on third-trimester PAS placenta) confirmed EGF expression differed significantly between invasion area, non-implantation area, and normal placenta (p<0.001); EGF concentrated in the non-implantation area, unlike VEGF-A which concentrated at the invasion site itself. No significant serum-vs-plasma difference was found.
>
> EGF is one of the serum biomarkers contributing to Model M2 (clinical + serum biomarkers), which reached AUC 0.942, sens 87.05%, spec 88.37% — outperforming both antenatal hemorrhage (65% detection) and ultrasonography (78% detection) in the same cohort.

**Response A** (diagnostic-performance / four-arm-design portion):

> EGF is elevated in PAS relative to placenta previa and preeclampsia across two independent cohorts, and is part of a combined clinical+serum model reaching AUC 0.942 — the best serum-only combined diagnostic model found in this wiki's corpus.
>
> Findings: ↑ Elevated — PAS-vs-PP ratio 1.70, PAS-vs-PE ratio 2.82 (both p<0.05); independent Cohort Two replication found ratio 3.03 vs. controls (p<0.01).
>
> This is a genuine four-arm design (Normal/PAS/placenta previa/preeclampsia), with placenta previa and preeclampsia enrolled as independent disease-control arms specifically to test biomarker specificity.
